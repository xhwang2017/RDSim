# RDSim
# Introduction
RDSim is a Python-Bash framework for generating biologically realistic synthetic cohorts with both genotypes and phenotypes. RDSim integrates 1000 Genomes haplotypes, curated pathogenic variants from ClinVar and Orphanet, and introduces novel de novo mutations, while modeling disease-specific Human Phenotype Ontology (HPO) annotations. Rare variant distributions are aligned with gnomAD to reflect real-world patterns. In a case study simulating a patient with achondroplasia, RDSim-generated data enabled Exomiser to correctly prioritize FGFR3 with high variant and phenotypic similarity scores. The framework supports autosomal dominant and recessive disorders, optional healthy controls, and customizable phenotype noise. RDSim helps enable systematic benchmarking, validation, and discovery in rare disease genomics.

RDSim is implemented as a pipeline containing Python and Bash modules. Python modules define the core data structures and algorithms for genotype and phenotype simulation, as well as input/output operations, while Bash scripts manage pipeline execution, and integration with external utilities. RDSim’s main modules make use of ProxyTyper for efficient sampling genotype data and generating background genotype simulation, and PLINK 2.0 for variant filtering, dataset merging, and binary genotype file generation, both of which are publicly available. 

For genotype simulation, the background genotypes were generated using the 1000 Genomes Project Phase 3 dataset as the reference panel, after which exome regions were extracted. Rare disease variants were curated from ClinVar and Orphanet, and novel variants were generated by modifying the reference allele from the hg19 FASTA sequence based on Ensembl GFF annotations. These variants were then introduced into selected individuals to produce the final simulated genotypes. Finally, rare variant frequencies in the simulated VCF were adjusted through histogram matching with the gnomAD VCF to accurately reflect the distribution of rare variants observed in real clinical genetic scenarios.

For phenotype simulation, HPO terms related to rare diseases, along with the complete set of HPO terms, are selected from the HPO database. Three scenarios can then be simulated for HPO term selection: using all HPO terms associated with the disease, randomly choosing n HPO terms related to rare diseases, or adding m HPO terms from the entire HPO database to introduce some noise.

# Generation of de novo mutations and alignment of rare variant frequency distributions in simulated exome VCFs with gnomAD.
We also introduce de novo mutations to represent the variability and complexity of variants observed in clinical genomics.

Rare variants are typically defined as genetic variants with a minor allele frequency (MAF) below 1% in the general population. The allele frequency spectrum (AFS) describes the distribution of allele frequencies within a genomic region and is generally enriched for very rare variants, the majority of which are singletons and doubletons (Klaudia et al. 2015). To ensure that our simulated genotypes reflect this distribution, we aligned the rare variant frequency distributions in the simulated exome genotype VCFs with those from gnomAD (v2.1.1, GRCh37), capturing real-world rare variant patterns.

# Evaluation 
To evaluate RDSim with Exomiser, we simulated exome data for a patient with achondroplasia (Orphanet code 15), an autosomal dominant skeletal disorder caused by germline mutations in FGFR3. 

<img width="2025" height="2277" alt="Figure3" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/816c484a-e53d-4c8d-afbe-eb9d0842cf7e" />

Fig. 3. Exomiser evaluation. A simulated patient carrying a de novo FGFR3 variant and presenting phenotypes including anteverted nares (HP:0000463), depressed nasal bridge (HP:0005280), obesity (HP:0001513), limb undergrowth (HP:0009826), and thoracolumbar kyphosis (HP:0005619) was analyzed using Exomiser. FGFR3 was ranked as the top-prioritized gene, with an Exomiser score of 0.993, a variant score of 0.984, and a phenotypic similarity score of 0.883, consistent with achondroplasia associated with FGFR3. The introduced novel variant (4-1807376-T-A) was successfully identified by Exomiser. 


# Functions
**1) Case-based simulation:** The simulation covers 1,508 rare diseases, including 694 autosomal dominant (AD) and 814 autosomal recessive (AR) conditions. Each rare disease patient has a mutation associated with a specific rare disease.

**2) Pair-based simulation:**  This allows for simulating 1,297 pairs of rare diseases (592 AD and 705 AR). Each pair is assigned the same Orphanet Code, with an associated variant added for each patient. In other words, the two patients in one pair will have different variants, all of which are causal for the disease. This simulated data can be used to match similar patients and identify genes associated with those matches.

**3) Pathway-based simlation:** This allows the simulation of 736 pathway-based rare diseases (342 AD and 394 AR). Each rare disease involves three patients: two as in the pair simulations, and a third who has a mutation in a gene within the same pathway as the causal gene. This approach can be used to match similar patients and identify new mutations in different genes associated with a disease.
